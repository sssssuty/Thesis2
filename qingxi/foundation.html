The proposal of a simplified traditional Chinese written language system was initiated by the National Party in 1935 to popularize fundamental education. The Communist Party soon adapted it, further developed it, and decide to use it as the official written language for the newborn People' Republic of China in 1949.

The Chinese government issued the first official Chinese Character Simplification Scheme in 1956, and in the following decades, with multiple rounds of revision, the GB2312 was published in 1980 May globally. GB2312 includes 6763 Simplified Chinese Characters and 682 non-Chinese characters and symbols.

Since the proposal was initiated to help foundamental education, all simplified characters are included in Xin Hua dictionary and published into testing since 1953. To 2016, the 11th version of Xin Hua dictionary debuted.
Pinyin is the Chinese version of the Internation Phonetic Alphabet which was build to help with pronunciation. The phrase "Pin Yin" literally translates into "spell sound."

There are 23 consonants 声母:
b, m, f, d, t, n, l, g, k, h, j, q, x, zh, ch, sh, r, z, c, s, y, w.

There are 24 vowels 韵母 including:
6 single vowels a, o, e, i, u, ü;
8 complex vowels ai, ei, ui, ao, ou, iu, ie, üe;
9 nasal vowels an, en, in, un, ün, ang, eng, ing, ong;
and 1 vowel that needs to curl the tongue 卷舌音 er;

There are also 4 accents highlighted as a "hat" on the single vowels: yin ping：－
